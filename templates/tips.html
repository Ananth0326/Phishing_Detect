Email Security Guide

Do's

  • Verify the sender's email.
  • Hover over links to preview the URL.
  • Contact the company directly if unsure.
  • Use two-factor authentication.
  • Report phishing emails to your provider.

Don'ts

  • Never click suspicious links.
  • Don't provide personal info via email.
  • Don't respond to urgent requests.
  • Never send passwords in an email.
  • Don't trust display names alone.

Common Phishing Tactics

Urgency & Fear

"Your account will be suspended!" or "Suspicious activity detected!"

Impersonation

Pretending to be your bank, a delivery service, or even your boss.

Greed & Curiosity

"You've won a prize!" or "Click to see this shocking video!"

What to Do If You're Phished

1. Change Passwords

Immediately change the password for compromised accounts, and any other accounts that use the same password.

2. Contact Financial Institutions

If financial information was involved, contact your bank and credit card companies to report fraud and freeze accounts.

3. Report the Incident

Report the phishing attempt to your IT department, your email provider, and relevant authorities like the Cyber Crime Portal.

4. Monitor Your Accounts

Keep a close eye on your accounts for any suspicious activity and consider setting up fraud alerts.
